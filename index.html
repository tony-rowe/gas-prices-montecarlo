Model

Prices follow an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck (mean-reverting log-normal) process:

d(ln P) = κ(μ − ln P)dt + σ dW

where κ is the reversion speed, μ the long-run mean, and σ the daily volatility. The log-normal formulation prevents negative prices while capturing the asymmetric distribution of fuel costs.

Scenario parameters

Escalation — Hormuz closure persists, crude stays elevated. Long-run pull toward ~$4.90, slow reversion (κ = 0.0035), higher volatility (σ = 0.025).

Base case — Partial normalization. Pull toward ~$3.72, moderate reversion (κ = 0.0055).

De-escalation — Ceasefire, crude retreats. Pull toward ~$3.05, fast reversion (κ = 0.0095), lower volatility (σ = 0.017).
